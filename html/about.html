In Chinese Pinyin, my last name is labeled as "L端", 端 is the same umlaut as in German or Turkish language. Back at the time I applied for passport, China didn't have a rule for how to spell this character, and when typing Chinese characters, people would type "lv" for it so a certain amount of people chose this form on their passports, others use "lu" instead.

Years later, Chinese government finally decided to unify the spelling of this character and neither of the forms above were used. "Lyu" is the official spelling of my last name now. So, this very last name has 3 different versions of English/Latin spelling: "Lyu", "Lu" and "Lv", how great is that?

According to Wikipedia, the pronunciation of 端 in Chinese Pinyin is similar as in German "端ber" or French "lune" (To pronounce this sound, say "ee" with rounded lips).
